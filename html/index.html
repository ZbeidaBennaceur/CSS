ABOUT

Zaha Hadid (Arabic: زها حديد), born on October 31, 1950, in Baghdad (Iraq), and passed away on March 31, 2016, in Miami (United States), was an Iraqi-British architect and urban planner. A prominent figure in the deconstructivist movement, she was one of the most awarded female architects in the profession. She received the Pritzker Prize (considered the "Nobel Prize of Architecture") in 2004.
Zaha Hadid was appointed Commander of the Order of the British Empire (CBE) in 2002, and later became Dame Commander (DBE) in 2012 for her services to architecture. In search of an alternative system to traditional architectural drawing, and influenced by Russian Suprematism and the avant-garde, Zaha Hadid used painting as a tool for design and abstraction as a principle of investigation to revisit untested and aborted modernist experiences, revealing new fields of construction. She was a pioneer in the use of parametric design to model her projects.

PROJECTS

Heydar Aliyev Center (Bakou, Azerbaïdjan)

Embark on an awe-inspiring architectural journey to the heart of Baku, the capital of Azerbaijan, where the Heydar Aliyev Center stands as a masterpiece of the architect. This avant-garde complex, much more than just a building, stands as a true symbol of the modernization and dynamism of post-Soviet Azerbaijan.
Far from the rigid and monumental constructions of the Soviet era, the Heydar Aliyev Center is distinguished by its fluid and dynamic shapes, resembling a crashing wave or a cascade of dunes. Its bold design, winner of an international competition in 2007, perfectly embodies the aspirations of a nation looking towards the future while paying tribute to Azerbaijan's rich culture.

Guangzhou Opera House (Canton, Chine)

Nicknamed the "giant egg shell" by the Cantonese, the Guangzhou Opera House, an extraordinary architectural masterpiece that defies the conventions of deconstructivism, stands at the heart of Guangzhou. This iconic monument of the new millennium, overlooking the majestic Pearl River, embodies the perfect convergence of sustainability, cutting-edge technology, and stunning aesthetics.

MAXXI – National Museum of the 21st Century Arts (Rome, Italy)

Forget traditional museums, confined within their walls and static collections. MAXXI, the National Museum of the 21st Century Arts in Rome, revolutionizes the museum experience by offering an innovative and bold architectural concept.
Imagine a field of buildings, accessible to all, where the boundaries between inside and outside blur. Converging lines, intersecting and separating walls, create a fluid and ever-evolving space, perfectly reflecting the dynamic and avant-garde spirit of the 21st century.
